01 — Mathematical Discussion

Theory & Derivation

Numerical integration estimates \(\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)\,dx\) when an analytical antiderivative is unavailable or impractical. The Composite Simpson's 1/3 Rule subdivides the interval into \(n\) equal parts (n must be even) and fits a quadratic (parabolic) polynomial through every consecutive triple of nodes, summing the exact areas under each parabola.

From Basic to Composite

For a single sub-interval \([a,b]\) with midpoint \(m\) and half-width \(h = (b-a)/2\), integrating the Lagrange quadratic interpolant through \(f(a)\), \(f(m)\), \(f(b)\) gives the basic rule:

Basic Simpson's 1/3
$$\int_a^b f(x)\,dx \approx \frac{h}{3}\bigl[f(a) + 4f(m) + f(b)\bigr]$$

Applying this over \(n/2\) consecutive pairs of sub-intervals of width \(h = (b-a)/n\) and collecting like terms yields:

Composite Simpson's 1/3 Rule
$$\int_a^b f(x)\,dx \approx \frac{h}{3}\Bigl[ f(x_0) + 4\!\sum_{\substack{i=1,3,5,\ldots}}^{n-1} f(x_i) + 2\!\sum_{\substack{i=2,4,6,\ldots}}^{n-2} f(x_i) + f(x_n) \Bigr]$$

where \(\;h = (b-a)/n\;\) and \(\;x_i = a + ih\)

Coefficient Pattern

Endpoints (i = 0, n)
Coefficient = 1
Odd indices (i = 1, 3, 5, …)
Coefficient = 4
Even interior (i = 2, 4, 6, …)
Coefficient = 2

Mnemonically, the weights follow the pattern 1 – 4 – 2 – 4 – 2 – … – 4 – 1.

Algorithm Steps

  1. Choose an even integer \(n \geq 2\) and compute \(h = (b-a)/n\).
  2. Generate nodes \(x_i = a + ih\) for \(i = 0, 1, \ldots, n\).
  3. Evaluate \(f(x_i)\) at every node.
  4. Multiply each \(f(x_i)\) by its coefficient (1, 4, or 2).
  5. Sum all weighted values, multiply by \(h/3\) — that is the integral approximation.

Error Analysis

Global Truncation Error
$$E \;=\; -\frac{(b-a)\,h^4}{180}\,f^{(4)}(\xi), \qquad \xi \in (a, b)$$

The method is fourth-order accurate: halving \(h\) (doubling \(n\)) reduces the error by \(2^4 = 16\times\). It integrates polynomials of degree ≤ 3 exactly.

Step-by-Step Solutions

Example 1 Integrating \(f(x) = x^4\) on \([0,\,2]\) with \(n = 4\)

Exact value: \(\displaystyle\int_0^2 x^4\,dx = \left[\tfrac{x^5}{5}\right]_0^2 = \tfrac{32}{5} = 6.4\)

Step 1 — Compute h and the nodes

$$h = \frac{b-a}{n} = \frac{2-0}{4} = 0.5$$

Nodes: \(x_0 = 0,\quad x_1 = 0.5,\quad x_2 = 1.0,\quad x_3 = 1.5,\quad x_4 = 2.0\)
